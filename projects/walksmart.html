Awards: WalkSmart won first place in the “robotics and intelligent machines” category at the 2023 Washington State Science and Engineering Fair, as well as the Naval Science Award from the U.S. Navy’s Office of Naval Research.

Abstract

Ambulatory fall incidents – which denote unanticipated falls during normal walking – are well known to be a leading cause of injury and injury-related deaths, especially in the elderly and in those with neurological disorders. Every year, one in four older adults experiences a fall, and every 11 seconds, someone is treated in an emergency department for an ambulatory fall-related injury. In this work, we aim to mitigate ambulatory falls by using smartphones to detect and alert people when their risk of falling increases. We propose WalkSmart – a new machine learning-based technique for ambulatory fall prevention based on analyzing gait variance using sensor data collected using smartphones. WalkSmart runs as a smartphone app and interacts with our cloud-based machine learning model to detect and alert people of impending falls, thereby potentially saving lives.

WalkSmart uses a new machine learning model for classifying gait patterns as steady or unsteady, using a long short-term memory (LSTM) based recurrent neural network as its basis. WalkSmart was trained using hours of real-world walking data collected using smartphones. Experiments show that it can effectively infer unsteady gait with a high accuracy of 96.6%, using a small window of accelerometer and gyroscope sensor data captured by a smartphone.
