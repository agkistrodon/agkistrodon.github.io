I am currently a freshman studying computer science at the University of Washington and an incoming Associate Software Development Intern at Google. I am interested in the areas of AI, cybersecurity, systems, and data visualization.

I am an undergraduate researcher with the Interactive Data Lab at the University of Washington. I am also a member of the Husky Satellite Lab, a Student Voices writer for the Seattle Times, and the Freshman Representative for the Women in Cybersecurity (WiCyS) Student Chapter at the University of Washington. I am also a NCWIT Aspirations in Computing National Honorable Mention designee as well as the NCWIT Seattle and Western Washington Affiliate Winner.

I was formerly a research intern at the Ubiquitous Computing Lab at the University of Washington, Washington Aerospace Scholar, a Johns Hopkins Advanced CTY member, the competitions coordinator for my high school Science Olympiad club, and a member of the National Honor Society.

I am passionate about building an interest in STEM among kids. I founded Stemity, a non profit through which I conduct STEM contests and workshops regularly. I have also worked as a summer counselor for Girls Rock Math.

In my spare time, I like to create digital art, develop video games, and read books!
